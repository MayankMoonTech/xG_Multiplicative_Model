Approach Used & Why — StatsBomb Multiplicative Model
▼
Data Source

StatsBomb Open Data (github.com/statsbomb/open-data)
~9,800 open-play shots from La Liga, Premier League, Champions League & World Cups.
Each shot has: location (x,y), outcome, under_pressure flag, and freeze-frame (all player positions at shot moment).

Step 1 — Base xG (Geography)

Map each shot's (x,y) to our 21×17 grid using:
gx = floor(x_sb / 6)
gy = floor(y_sb / 5)
Then: base xG = goals ÷ shots in that zone.
Zone 1 = 42% conversion. Zone 14 = 1%.

Step 2 — Press Multiplier

From StatsBomb under_pressure field + freeze-frame defender count.
Free shots convert at 1.35× average. Under 3+ defenders: 0.52×.
Reference: Robberechts et al. (2019) Valuing on-the-ball actions.

Step 3 — Balance Multiplier

From freeze-frame: count attackers with x_sb > 100, 18 < y_sb < 62 at shot moment.
More attackers = keeper distracted, rebounds, defenders displaced.
Reference: Spearman (2018) Beyond Expected Goals.

Why multiplicative, not logistic regression?
Independence: Geography, press, and balance are approximately orthogonal — their effects multiply, not add. A free shot from Zone 14 is not dangerous even with 11 attackers.
Interpretability: Coaching staff can understand "Zone 1 base × press penalty × balance boost" immediately. Logistic regression coefficients require more context.
Updatable: When new zone data arrives, update one number. Regression requires full retraining.
Constraint-safe: Product stays in [0,1] without additional sigmoid transformation needed.
